Kenneth M. Falgoust

Researcher focused on International Relations theory, political psychology, and civil discourse.

Partisan America, Non-Partisan Americans
Published in Essais, Vol. 15, No. 2 (Fall 2025)
Abstract: This article addresses the escalation of political violence in the U.S. through the lens of "affective polarization"; the view that animosity is driven by negative emotions rather than policy disagreement. By analyzing the issue through Kenneth Burke’s theory of identification and combing it with Alexis de Tocqueville’s observations on voluntary association, the research argues that Americans can mitigate hostility by prioritizing shared, apolitical identities.
